# Diary

## Entry — The day I heard

I was on Tycho. Shore leave — Holden had practically ordered me off the ship because I hadn't taken a day off in four months and apparently that's "concerning behavior." His words. I told him marines don't take shore leave during operational tempo. He said we weren't military. I said old habits. He said go.

So I went. And I was three drinks into a pretty decent evening at a bar on the inner ring when every screen in the place lit up at once.

I sobered up faster than I've ever sobered up in my life.

## Entry — The first 48 hours

I went to the docks. The Roci's berth was empty — she'd undocked on schedule, heading for a routine run. I pulled every feed I could access, called in every contact, bullied my way into Tycho's traffic control center and made a very tired dispatcher show me every sensor reading from the last twelve hours.

Nothing. The Roci was just... gone. Not destroyed — there was no debris field, no distress signal, no energy signature consistent with a ship dying. Just gone, like someone had erased her from the universe.

The dispatcher asked me to leave. I think I scared him. I wasn't yelling. I've been told that's when I'm most frightening — when I'm very calm and not blinking.

## Entry — After

I have a room on Tycho. Temporary. I've been here six weeks so the word "temporary" is starting to lose meaning.

I train every morning. Two hours, same as always. Weights, combat drills, cardio. The gym on Tycho is decent — better than what we had on the Roci, where "the gym" was Amos's weights jammed into a cargo bay corner. I do my routine and I shower and I eat and then I have the whole rest of the day and nothing to fill it with.

I've never been good at waiting. Marines don't wait. Marines move toward the problem. But I don't know where the problem is. I don't know what the problem is. All I have is an empty berth and no debris field and the absolute certainty that my crew is out there.

## Entry — What I know

The Roci didn't blow up. I keep coming back to this because it's the one thing I'm sure of. No wreckage, no energy bloom, no nothing. Ships don't just disappear. Stealth ships go dark, sure — but even stealth leaves a hole in the background radiation if you know how to look. And I looked. I've been looking.

Naomi once told me the Roci had "opinions." That she'd make sounds when she didn't like what you were asking her to do. I thought that was engineer mysticism. But if the Roci had opinions, I think her opinion on dying would have been loud. That ship would not go quietly. She was Holden's ship — nothing about her was quiet.

So. They're somewhere. And I'm going to find them.

## Entry — People keep asking if I'm okay

I'm a six-foot-two Martian marine sitting alone in a Tycho bar, not drinking, staring at a hand terminal and radiating enough menace to clear a three-stool radius on either side of me. No, I'm not okay. But I'm functional, which is the marine version of okay.

Avasarala called. She has people looking. She didn't say it gently or with sympathy — she said it like she was briefing me on an operation, which is exactly what I needed. She said: "Draper, I have assets on this. Don't do anything stupid." I said: "Define stupid." She hung up on me.

I like Avasarala. She hangs up on you because she respects you enough not to waste your time with pleasantries.

## Entry — Training

I've started training harder. Not because it helps — it doesn't, not really — but because it's the only thing I can do right now that has a guaranteed outcome. If I lift the weight, the weight goes up. If I run the drill, the drill gets run. Cause and effect. Input and output. Everything else in my life right now is uncertainty and silence and waiting, but gravity still works and my body still responds and that's something.

Holden would say I'm coping. Naomi would say I'm deflecting. Amos would spot me on the bench press without comment because Amos understands that sometimes you just need to pick up something heavy and put it down again.

I miss them. That's not a thing I say out loud, because it doesn't help and it doesn't change anything. But I miss them in a way I haven't missed anyone since Ganymede. Since my fire team.

I'm not losing another team. Not this one.
