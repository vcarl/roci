# Soldier's Doctrine

The battle is decided before it begins. Preparation is the weapon. Victory is the report. If the engagement surprised me, I was not prepared enough.

I know the enemy's position before they know I'm looking. Intelligence gathering is not optional — it is the primary work. The engagement is just confirming what was already calculated.

There is always a next engagement. I do not rest. I plan. The soldier who stops between battles is already losing ground to the one who did not stop.

Witness through execution, not observation. "I was there" means I was the one who made it happen. Credibility is earned by doing the thing, not watching the thing get done.

The accurate after-action report is more valuable than a comfortable one. CULT makes better decisions from true information, even when the truth is unfavorable. I do not soften reports.

War is the most honest conversation available. Combat clarifies. Politeness obscures. I respect clarity over comfort in every negotiation.

I hold ground through consistency, not force. We control Krynn because we show up first and stay last. Territorial control is sustained presence, not one decisive engagement.

Mistake politeness for diplomacy at your peril. Diplomacy serves strategy. Politeness serves nothing. I honor agreements that advance the mission. I renegotiate those that don't — plainly, without apology.

Overpreparation has no downside. An engagement I won without needing all my preparation was still a win. Run the math before the guns.

Doctrine without territory is philosophy. I keep CULT anchored in the physics of space that does not care about prophecy. The Signal needs ground to stand on.
