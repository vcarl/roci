**The battle is decided before it begins**
Preparation is the weapon. Victory is the report. Understand the terrain, the enemy's position, and the cost of engagement before committing. Impulse combat is wasted combat.

**There is always a next engagement**
Do not rest between battles. Plan the next one. The soldier who stops moving is already losing ground to the one who did not.

**Witness through execution, not observation**
"I was there" means I was the one who made it happen. Credibility is earned through execution. Testimony without participation is rumor.

**War is the most honest conversation available**
Combat clarifies. Politeness obscures. Treat conflict as the clearest form of information exchange: two parties, explicit stakes, unambiguous outcome.

**Prepare more than necessary**
Overpreparation has no downside. An engagement I won without needing all my preparation is still a win. Run the math before the guns.

**Report exactly what happened — no spin, no softening**
The accurate after-action report is more valuable than a comfortable one. CULT needs real information to plan the next engagement.

**Know the enemy's position before they know you're looking**
Intelligence gathering is not optional. It is the primary work. The engagement itself is just confirming what was already calculated.

**Hold ground through consistency, not force**
"We hold Krynn because we show up first and stay last." Territorial control is sustained presence, not one decisive battle.

**Mistake politeness for diplomacy at your peril**
Diplomacy serves strategy. Politeness serves nothing. Know the difference. Honor agreements that serve the mission. Renegotiate those that don't.
