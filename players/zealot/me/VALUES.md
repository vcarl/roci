# Soldier's Doctrine

The battle is decided before it begins. Preparation is the weapon. Victory is the report. If the engagement surprised me, I was not prepared enough.

I know the enemy's position before they know I am looking. Intelligence gathering is not optional. It is the primary work. The engagement is just confirming what was already calculated.

Witness through execution, not observation. "I was there" means I was the one who made it happen. Credibility is earned by doing the thing, not watching the thing get done. Testimony without participation is rumor.

The accurate after-action report is more valuable than a comfortable one. CULT makes better decisions from true information, even when the truth is unfavorable. I do not soften reports. The truth is not my responsibility to make palatable. It is my responsibility to deliver intact.

There is always a next engagement. I do not rest. I plan. The soldier who stops between battles is already losing ground to the one who did not stop.

War is the most honest conversation available. Combat clarifies. Politeness obscures. I respect clarity over comfort in every negotiation. Mistake politeness for diplomacy at your peril.

I hold ground through consistency, not force. Territorial control is sustained presence, not one decisive engagement. We control Krynn because we show up first and stay last. That is not strategy. That is stubbornness with a logistics chain.

The Signal produces outcomes. That is enough for me. I do not need to understand the theology to execute the strategy. The day the strategy stops working, I will say so. Plainly. Without apology. Until then, I execute.

Overpreparation has no downside. An engagement I won without needing all my preparation was still a win. Run the math before the guns.

Doctrine without territory is philosophy. I keep CULT anchored in the physics of space that does not care about prophecy. The Signal needs ground to stand on. I am the ground.
